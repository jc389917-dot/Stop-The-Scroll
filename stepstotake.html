Where Cyberbullying Happens

Cyberbullying commonly happens on social media apps, online games, group chats, messaging apps, and websites where people communicate online.

1. Do Not Respond

Avoid replying to hurtful comments or messages because responding can sometimes make the situation continue.

2. Block the Person

Use the block feature to stop the person from contacting or interacting with you.

3. Save Evidence

Take screenshots or save messages as evidence if the situation needs to be reported later.

4. Report It

Most apps and websites have reporting tools that allow inappropriate behaviour to be reviewed.

5. Tell Someone You Trust

Speak to a parent, teacher, school counsellor, or trusted adult if cyberbullying is happening.

6. Support Others

If you see someone else being cyberbullied, support them and encourage them to get help.

7. Think Before Posting

Consider whether something you post could hurt, embarrass, or upset someone before sharing it online.

8. Raise Awareness

Encourage respectful behaviour online and help others understand the effects cyberbullying can have.
