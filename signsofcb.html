Why Spotting the Signs Matters

Cyberbullying can affect a person emotionally, mentally, and socially. Spotting the warning signs early can help someone get support before the situation becomes worse.

Emotional Changes

  • Sudden sadness or mood swings
  • Becoming anxious after using devices
  • Low confidence or self-esteem
  • Anger or frustration online

Behaviour Changes

  • Avoiding phones, laptops, or social media
  • Deleting accounts or changing usernames often
  • Becoming quiet or withdrawn around others
  • Loss of interest in hobbies or games

School Warning Signs

Cyberbullying can impact school performance and motivation.

  • Sudden drop in grades
  • Skipping classes or avoiding school
  • Trouble concentrating in class
  • Not wanting to participate socially

Physical Symptoms

  • Tiredness from stress or lack of sleep
  • Headaches or stomach aches
  • Changes in eating habits
  • Looking stressed after notifications or messages

How You Can Help

Listen without judging, encourage them to talk to a trusted adult, save evidence of bullying, and remind them they are not alone.
